Online Classroom Tracking

Designing a online teaching tool for teachers to track classroom progress.

Role

Product design lead

Tools

Figma, Usertesting.com

Timeline

6 weeks

Team

Product manager, engineer, design manager

Schoolhouse is an education initiative started by Sal Khan to help provide quality, free online education to anyone and everyone who wants to learn.

In online classrooms, teachers use Schoolhouse to evaluate their students. Students complete Schoolhouse certifications to demonstrate their understanding of course units.

Who is our primary user?

Our primary user is a teacher using Schoolhouse as an online teaching resource. Teachers have to track the certification progress of every student in their class, which is difficult in a remote learning environment.

Problem

The current method of tracking certification progress is inefficient. Teachers are not aware of updates when students complete new certifications.

Solution

Classrooms

Teachers create classrooms for students to join so data from all students in the class is consolidated.

Certification status table

The certification table displays certification statuses for each student in the course and is consistently updated.

Detailed student view

Sometimes, there are students that struggle more than others. To obtain more insight into the needs of their students, teachers can view individual details for each student.

Outcome

The MVP was launched in August of 2022. The tool will be piloted with teachers at Khan World School in Fall 2022, a full-time online school of 200 students from around the world.

Learnings

Discovery

I joined this project after research and discovery. The project team already talked to teachers at Khan World School about their current experience. It was found that in order for teachers to view certification progress, they would request Schoolhouse transcripts from every student indvidually.

Additionally, new transcripts had to be requested every time a student completed a certification, but teachers had no indication as to when new updates occured.

Design goals

Consolidate classroom data for teachers so monitoring a class’ certification progress is more efficient.
Keep teachers informed about updated certification statuses.

Early designs

Iconography

New icons were required for this project. There were two main considerations: Symbol and Table Row Grouping. Icon color was already defined in the Schoolhouse design system.

1 — Symbol

How can we represent certification statuses?

The Schoolhouse design system used font-awesome icons, but several options were curated for consideration.

Option 5 was chosen because it was most cohesive. The “checkmark” complemented the “x” to represent two opposing statuses and the hourglass best represented a pending state, which required both the student and teacher to wait for their certification to be reviewed. Option 7 was the most straightforward representation but would ultimately overwhelm the certification table.

How can we represent completed course vs. unit certifications??

Font-awesome icons had limitations and appropriate symbols were not found for course and unit certifications, so I made two custom icons. A unit is a subset of a course and their symbols should be cohesive and representative of this relationship.

2 — Table Row Grouping

Option 3 was chosen because the square icons created cohesion and increased contrast between statuses. The “Not Started” status was left without a square background to reduce clutter and create visual constrast for this status. Coloring the cell background additionally made statuses easier to scan within the table, which is important for the user.

Design considerations

How might we consolidate classroom data for teachers?

Option 1
Strengths

  • Hierarchy is established between two actions

  • All classrooms that are relevant to the user are visible

Tradeoffs

  • It is unlikely for teachers to join classrooms, resulting in an indefinite empty state

Option 2
Strengths

  • Required actions are made clear by input field

  • Accordion hides joined classes from teachers and creates additional screen space

Tradeoffs

  • Although hidden when collapsed, the accordion is an area of dead screen space that remains unused by teachers

Option 3
Strengths

  • Use cases are differentiated

  • Tabs are labelled with clear call-to-action

Tradeoffs

  • Students who need to join a class must first navigate themselves to the correct tab

Final decision

Option 3 was used because it was important for student/teacher use cases to be differentiated. ‘Joining a classroom’ should not be reduced to a secondary action because for students, this is their primary action.

Design considerations

How might we keep teachers informed about updated certification statuses?

Option 1
Strengths

  • Familiar to Khan Academy users

  • No truncation of unit names

Tradeoffs

  • Accordions are inpractical

  • Information is difficult to scan

Option 2
Strengths

  • No overflow on table content

  • Table content is easy to scan

Tradeoffs

  • Some unit names are truncated

  • Unit names are difficult to read

  • Not easy to develop

Option 3
Strengths

  • Familiar to teacher gradebooks

  • Unit names are easy to read

  • Table content is easy to scan

Tradeoffs

  • Some unit names are truncated

  • Table content overflows and horizontal scrolling is required

Final decision

Option 3 was used because it was important for student/teacher use cases to be differentiated. ‘Joining a classroom’ should not be reduced to a secondary action because for students, this is their primary action.
